Staying in control of our lives can be a daily struggle—especially this year, and especially for the half of us who live with a chronic condition.* It’s time for a change. It’s time to work together to take back control. It’s time to focus on our health again. And it’s time for us to get back to living.

Many people struggle with motivation for self-care. That’s normal, so if everything hasn’t been going perfectly, don’t be hard on yourself. With a few small tweaks, you can take real steps towards better self-care without even leaving the house..

Breathe. Just breathe.

Living with a chronic condition isn’t easy, so have mercy on yourself. Instead of comparing yourself to others, try comparing yourself to who you were yesterday, and who you’d like to be tomorrow

Staying connected to each other is essential. It’s true with our personal lives, our professional lives, and certainly with our health. Your friends and neighbors can help you stay on track—and in turn, you can inspire them through your healthy actions.

Tell your story

If you have a success story to share for others with your condition, even if it’s small, share it. Did you manage 10,000 steps today? Did you drink all the water you wanted to? You never know who may need to hear about it. And remember, when you’re struggling, try not to compare yourself to others. You can always ask for help—from a friend, doctor, patient organization, or whoever makes you comfortable.

Remember how much you have to give

You are far more than your chronic condition. In fact, your experiences could be invaluable to those facing similar health challenges. However you can help, you might be surprised by how good it feels to give back.

Re-engage at your own pace.

With communities gradually reopening, your neighborhood may look quite different than it did even a month ago. Look at this as an opportunity to define your new reality.
